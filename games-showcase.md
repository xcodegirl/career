# Published Games - Silicon Hanna Inc. / SuRJE Software

A showcase of the mobile games I designed, built, and shipped to the App Store and Google Play between 2008 and 2020, first under SuRJE Software Solutions Inc. and later under Silicon Hanna Inc. I co-founded both with Ricardo Hoar and worked as lead developer: implementation, engine and SDK research, graphics, physics, and release. Eleven headline titles shipped commercially, along with holiday and themed spin-offs (Santa's Loot, Crypto Scripture); two more (Climate Crisis and Cubey) remain in development and unreleased. The descriptions below draw on the studio's own press releases and promo sheets.

## The games

### Ragin' Bull (soft-launched December 2020; full release February 2021; iOS and Android)

The studio's first 3D title, built in Unreal Engine. Instead of controlling a human character, you become a 3D bull smashing your way through the world, collecting points and power-ups and bursting into a raging buck. The game deliberately omits guns and gore for a more lighthearted, cathartic experience: you smash hundreds of items, from layered cakes and mailboxes to carousels and barrels, across 11 unique levels and over a dozen different shops and streets, complete with hidden surprises, a pirate battle, and a high-score achievement board on both platforms. Beautiful music, artwork, and sound effects sit on top of realistic, responsive physics.

It began as a COVID-era project rooted in the founders' Southern Alberta cowboy heritage: the idea was, wouldn't it be awesome if you could be the bull, escape the rodeo, and smash the town. It was designed to appeal to casual gamers, women, teens, and anyone who thinks being a Bull in a China Shop sounds fun.

App Store listing id 1506244627. Google Play id com.siliconhanna.RaginBull. Studio page at siliconhanna.com/ragin-bull.

### AR Picture Portal (2018, iOS)

An ARKit app for placing real-sized images and illusions in your own space. Look around your room and tap to place pictures on walls and tables, with a control pillar to manage albums. Long-tap a photo to launch walk-in images and illusions, and host or join a friend's session for a multiplayer AR experience.

App Store listing id 1378502780. Studio slug telepic-portal.

### Fragmented Reality (2018, iOS)

An augmented-reality music toy: a set of AR instruments you strum, tap, and play in your living room. Musical cubes and fractal wind chimes are tuned to chords; you pick a chord, place the instruments, and play the notes to build harmonies and melodies in key. The wind chimes use fractal math to generate 3D shapes that double as instruments, with original soundscapes in keys and tempos of your choosing.

App Store listing id 1376533564. (Published as "Fragmented Reality"; earlier resume drafts said "Fragment Reality", now standardized.)

### Ice Cream AR (2018, iOS)

A lighthearted ARKit and SceneKit casual game layered onto your real environment. Unlock cones and flavours, then run around your real room stacking cones for customers who order at virtual tables placed atop your real ones. A real 3D physics world means spilled ice cream splats and bounces off your actual furniture, dripping down the couch and onto the floor.

### Jewel Slide (2017, iOS; 2019, Android)

A sliding-tile puzzle for all ages. Match jewels, opals, pearls, and other items by sliding rows and columns horizontally and vertically; tiles that slide off one edge wrap around to the other side.

App Store listing id 522527960.

### Word Slide and Find (2017, iOS)

A word puzzle built on the same sliding mechanic. Rearrange a square of tiles by sliding rows and columns to find hidden items, unlock classical composers, and travel the world through your window.

App Store listing id 525328139.

### A Word Theme (2017, iOS; 2019, Android)

A themed word puzzle in the studio's sliding-tile family, pairing word play with the slide mechanic.

App Store listing id 542745167.

### CryptoQuip (2011, iOS; 2019, Android)

An authentic reproduction of the classic cryptogram puzzle: decode famous quotes by dragging and dropping alphabet tiles from your stock onto the board. Shipped in free (ad-supported) and paid editions. One of the studio's earliest and longest-lived titles.

App Store listing id 428848389.

### Crypto Scripture (2017, iOS): spin-off of CryptoQuip

A scripture-themed variant of CryptoQuip using the same drag-and-drop cryptogram mechanic, with puzzles drawn from scripture rather than general quotes.

App Store listing id 1317531229.

### Rafter's Loot (2011, iOS)

A fast-paced 2D physics game built with OpenGL and the Chipmunk physics engine. You control a raft and catch the jetsam loot thrown from merchant and pirate ships, working through multiple islands to collect different kinds of loot, with Game Center high scores. Free to download and play, with an in-app option to remove ads. The gameplay is built around real physics:

- Scoring: keep loot on your raft until the end of the level to score it, or touch three of a kind to clear and score them immediately.
- Rope: your load sways realistically as you move; tie items down with rope (earned through play, by watching videos, or via in-app purchase) to keep them aboard.
- Pirates and explosions: pirates throw TNT that bursts your loot crates, spilling the "mini loot" inside, which you tap to score before it spoils.
- Monsters: creatures from the deep emerge to rock the raft, so navigating past them takes timing.
- Multiple rafts: unlock and upgrade rafts with rope and collected coin to carry larger loads.

App Store listing id 455760896.

### Santa's Loot (2011, iOS): holiday spin-off of Rafter's Loot

A festive seasonal version of Rafter's Loot, carrying the same raft-and-physics loot gameplay into a holiday theme.

App Store listing id 476477492.

### Star Chase (2008, iOS)

An early SuRJE arcade title and a genuinely cutting-edge use of the iPhone's accelerometer in the studio's first year on the App Store. You flung planets into orbit around stars, then tilted the device to steer gravity and keep them in orbit. Built on the OpenGL and Chipmunk 2D physics foundation, with tilt-based controls at a time when sensor-driven gameplay was brand new on mobile.

### Bitsy (2008, iOS)

A simple toddler game from the studio's first year, built on the same OpenGL and Chipmunk 2D foundation. It looped "Itsy Bitsy Spider" while toddlers tapped to send a spider around a small 2D world collecting fruit. A modest early title, but part of the studio's first wave of App Store releases.

## In development (unreleased)

### Climate Crisis

The studio's most ambitious concept: a historical open-world game rooted in real Alberta heritage. You play as John Ware, one of Alberta's most celebrated Black ranchers and homesteaders, or as his wife Mildred, building a homestead on the Alberta foothills. From that starting point the game spans eras: you invent and discover new sources of energy and work to evade the mounting consequences of climate change as each era unfolds. The pairing of grounded Alberta history with a climate-and-energy arc across time made it one of the studio's most original designs.

Status: development paused, unreleased.

### Cubey

A 3D educational open-world game teaching beginner math and mental math tricks. The world expands progressively as you explore: more map opens up the further you go, with math levels and units gating progression. The central visual mechanic is the cube representation: characters and quantities can be shown either as digits or as physical stacks of cubes, letting players see at a glance the difference between big and small numbers. The cube stacks make abstract quantity concrete in a way that works naturally in a 3D world. Additional math units were in research when development paused.

Status: development paused, unreleased.

## Technology by era

- 2008 to 2011 (SuRJE): native iOS in Objective-C, with 2D physics gameplay built on OpenGL and Chipmunk (Star Chase, Bitsy, Rafter's Loot and its Santa's Loot spin-off, early CryptoQuip).
- 2017 to 2019 (Silicon Hanna, puzzle line): native iOS and Android word and sliding-tile games (Jewel Slide, Word Slide and Find, A Word Theme, the Crypto Scripture variant), with AdMob and in-app purchases.
- 2018 (AR line): ARKit and SceneKit augmented-reality apps (AR Picture Portal, Fragmented Reality, Ice Cream AR), including real-world physics and fractal-math visuals.
- 2020 (3D line): Unreal Engine for the studio's first fully 3D open-world title (Ragin' Bull).
